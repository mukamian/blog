With all my regards to ma’am my eyes turned heavy with every topic she taught
I had interest so decided to feel every topic as studying was what I ought
Soon all unrecognized voices faded but I still heard a crowd
Distracted from the topic, with all queries I felt the disbelieve when I figured it out
Similar faces whom I once said good bye someday long were back around
Nothing had changed the condition, neither had aged what surrounds
With a surprise like I just woke up from a long dream I walked around like sane
Noticing, feeling everything I remember once thought won’t be able to see again
The undecorated soft board, green board with date T.S., P.S., A.S., and the topic explained
The lost windowpanes, duster, those dismantled desks with different name on plain
A group in the corner whose chuckles echoes the room
A group at first in first row discussing of who said to whom
Those boys in last of the mid row where in some deep thoughts
But I, I just walk down to the desk I remember as mine, no notice I got
And I saw a mirror, the reflection was more alive and laughing with the guys near
I remember them, not just by name but those guys I know as someone who always care
Laughing listening to the jokes I could predict would end like but still no one sensed I’m there
Seeing the desk top painted with lyrics of all the songs I knew we would sing here
Then when the reflection of mine walked by I was so sure where it was heading to
So I walked down in that lonely corridor leading to the places I been before to
But soon a touch at shoulder blurred that corridor and light come through a half
I opened my eyes, hearing them giggling and laugh
But ma’am stood still and her eyes raised with query of my dosing
And the floor grew rough as I kept on stare might be causing
The minute hand swung and turned to be a savior
The voice that faded reminded me that was a bad behavior
The people around had different laugh, no soft board neither the seat I draw as mine
That was the time I have been living in so realized to be fine
