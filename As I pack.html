All my life we spent underneath this roof
Never thought of this day, far or soon
It feels a lifetime ago
See today I got the work and thus have to go
Looking into the cupboard left with things I thought will ever find
They brought up the memories of a very different kind
So sat on the chair I discovered today
Under the pile of clothes, I wore in the day
Sitting idle brought attention to the wall
Thinking of the fight we had for the color, right there in the hall
But now it too feels like a pitcher
The studio of our own and thus in every picture
We living together did hit hard in our privacy
But that just brought us to close no matter who tries to seek
The floor still has very awful stains of what ate and spilled
And those windows still have the marks of the tears when the heart was filled
Every laugh every joke now echoes as I pack
I would miss this place and wish that time could ever bring me back
